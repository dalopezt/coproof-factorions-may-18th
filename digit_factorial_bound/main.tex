**Theorem.** Let $n$ be a natural number with $n \geq 1$. Then the sum of the factorials of the digits of $n$ (in base 10) satisfies $$\sum_{d \,\in\, \text{digits}(n)} d! \;\leq\; 362880 \cdot (\lfloor \log_{10} n \rfloor + 1).$$

*Proof.* The number of base-10 digits of $n$ equals $\lfloor \log_{10} n \rfloor + 1$. Each digit $d$ of $n$ satisfies $0 \leq d \leq 9$, and the largest factorial among these is $9! = 362880$. Therefore every term in the sum is at most $362880$, and since there are exactly $\lfloor \log_{10} n \rfloor + 1$ terms, the total sum is bounded above by $362880 \cdot (\lfloor \log_{10} n \rfloor + 1)$. $\blacksquare$
